title Grad-B Drift

In a non-uniform magnetic field, charged particles
drift perpendicular to both B and its gradient.
Electrons and ions drift in opposite directions.

Watch the electron's orbit carefully. Besides the usual
gyration, the particle drifts sideways. This is the
grad-B drift: the gyroradius is smaller where B is
stronger and larger where B is weaker, causing a net
displacement per orbit.

The grad-B drift velocity is:
v_D = (mv_perp^2 / 2qB^2) * (B x grad B) / B

Key features: it is perpendicular to both B and
grad(B), and it depends on the sign of the charge.
Electrons and protons drift in opposite directions.
